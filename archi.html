Introduction

BMW, a German luxury car and motorcycle manufacturer, traces its origins back to 1916 as Bayerische Flugzeug-Werke AG (BFW), a builder of aircraft engines. In 1917, the company changed its name to Bayerische Motoren Werke (BMW) and began producing motorcycles in the 1920s. BMW entered the automobile business in 1928 and has since become a global leader in the premium automotive and motorcycle market. .

BMW S1000 RR

BMW S1000RR is a race orientated sport bike initially made by BMW Motorrad to compete in the 2009 Superbike World Championship, that is now in commercial production. It was introduced in Munich in April 2008, and is powered by a 999 cc transverse inline four-cylinder engine redlined at 14,200 rpm.

  • On road price: From 20.7 lakhs
  • Max speed: 299 to 303 km/h
  • Curb weight: 193.5 to 210 kg
  • Seat height: 824 mm
  • Displacement: 999 cc
  • Fuel economy: 15.6 km/l
  • Color options: Black Storm Metallic, Style Passion, Red, Blue, Light White/Racing Blue Metallic/Racing Red

BMW G 1200 GS

The BMW R1200GS and R1200GS Adventure are motorcycles manufactured in Berlin, Germany by BMW Motorrad, part of the BMW group. It is one of the BMW GS family of dual sport motorcycles. Both motorcycles have a 1,170 cc, two-cylinder boxer engine with four valves per cylinder.

  • On road price: From 17.3 lakhs
  • Curb weight: 244 kg
  • Max speed: 200 km/h
  • Displacement: 1,170 cc
  • Fuel tank capacity: 20 L
  • Seat height: 870 mm
  • Fuel economy: 16 to 19 km/l
